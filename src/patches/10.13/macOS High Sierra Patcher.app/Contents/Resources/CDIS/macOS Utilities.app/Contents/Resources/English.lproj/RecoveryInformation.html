Recovery information

This document contains information about repairing your computer’s volume, reinstalling macOS while keeping your files and user settings intact, using Time Machine to restore your computer’s system, and erasing and reinstalling macOS.

For more help, go to https://www.apple.com/support.

Connect to the Internet

If you need to connect to the Internet, you can use a wireless or wired network connection. To join a wireless network:

  1. Click the Wi-Fi status icon in the menu bar (upper-right corner of the screen), then choose a network.
  2. If necessary, type the network’s password.

To join a closed network, choose Join Other Network. Enter the network’s name and, if necessary, the network’s password.

Repair your volume

If you have issues with your computer or if you started your computer and saw the macOS Utilities pane, you may need to repair your computer’s internal storage device and volume.

Note:You may want to write down these instructions before closing this window or quitting Safari.

  1. Choose Safari > Quit Safari, select Disk Utility in the macOS Utilities pane, then click Continue.
  2. Select the internal storage device in the list on the left, then click First Aid.
  3. Select the startup volume in the list on the left, then click First Aid again.
  4. Click Run.

If Disk Utility can’t repair your disk, back up as much of your information as possible, then follow the instructions in “Erase and reinstall macOS” later in this document.

Reinstall macOS

Under some circumstances, you may need to reinstall macOS. You can reinstall macOS while keeping your files and user settings intact.

Note:You may want to write down these instructions before closing this window or quitting Safari.

  1. Choose Safari > Quit Safari.
  2. Make sure you’re connected to the Internet. (To connect to a wireless network, follow the instructions in “Connect to the Internet” earlier in this document.)
  3. Select Reinstall macOS, then click Continue.
  4. In the pane where you select a volume, select your current macOS volume (in most cases, it is the only one available).
  5. Click Customize to select or deselect optional software.
  6. Click Install.

Use Time Machine to restore your system

If you erase and reinstall macOS and you previously created a Time Machine backup, use the Restore utility to restore everything on your computer to its previous condition.

Only use your Time Machine backup to restore information to the computer that is the source of the backup. If you want to transfer information to a new computer, use Migration Assistant (in the Other folder in Launchpad).

Note:You may want to write down these instructions before closing this window or quitting Safari.

  1. Choose Safari > Quit Safari.
  2. If your backup is on a Time Capsule, make sure you’re connected to the Internet. (To connect to a wireless network, follow the instructions in “Connect to the Internet” earlier in this document.)
  3. Select Restore From Time Machine Backup, then click Continue.
  4. Select a backup source, select the volume that contains the Time Machine backup, then follow the onscreen instructions.

Erase and reinstall macOS

If you erase and reinstall macOS, everything (your user accounts, network settings, and all of your files and folders) is deleted. Before you erase macOS, back up any files you want to keep by copying them to another volume. Write down your network settings from Network preferences to make it easier to get connected again after reinstalling macOS.

Note:You may want to write down these instructions before closing this window or quitting Safari.

  1. Choose Safari > Quit Safari.
  2. Make sure you’re connected to the Internet. (To connect to a wireless network, follow the instructions in “Connect to the Internet” earlier in this document.)
  3. Select Disk Utility, then click Continue.
  4. Select your volume in the list on the left, then click Erase.
  5. Enter a name for your volume, choose Mac OS Extended from the Format pop-up menu, then click Erase.

    After the volume has been erased, choose Disk Utility > Quit Disk Utility. Select Reinstall macOS, click Continue, then follow the instructions in the macOS Installer to reinstall macOS.
